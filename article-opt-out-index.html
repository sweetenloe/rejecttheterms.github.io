The Opt-Out Index

A living page for documenting opt-outs, buried controls, and the clauses that push too far.

← Back to articles

This page tracks opt-out paths, privacy exits, and the terms that deserve closer reading. Each entry is meant to stay practical: the company, the source links, the language to watch, and the account or privacy controls that matter most.

How this page works

  • Each company gets direct source links to its terms and privacy pages.
  • Questionable clauses are quoted directly once reviewed.
  • Notes focus on what feels coercive, invasive, misleading, or overly broad.
  • Whenever possible, opt-out, deletion, and privacy-control links are listed nearby.

Review criteria

  • Overly broad rights to collect, infer, retain, or share personal data.
  • Forced arbitration, class-action waiver, venue restrictions, or asymmetric remedies.
  • One-sided license grabs over user content, uploads, prompts, or account materials.
  • Dark-pattern friction around deletion, opt-out, tracking controls, or account closure.
  • Terms that can change unilaterally with weak notice or vague consent language.
